In the space between breaths you see the shape of it burned into your retinas, 

if only for a moment.

You try to conjure it again, watching it unravel into new shapes 

and spill its guts like a sock filled with spaghetti noodles.

You have forgotten something. 

You will not be able to retrieve this shape; touch it, see it, feel it.

And as you sit with this uncomfortable knowing, you open a journal, a document, a note-taking app, 

and you write some hazy adjectives. 

You swear, next time, you will write it down before you forget.
